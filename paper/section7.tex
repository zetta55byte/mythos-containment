\section{Representative Empirical Validation}
\label{sec:eval}

\subsection{Scope and framing}

The Mythos-Class Containment Architecture (MCCA) makes three central
architectural claims that require empirical grounding: (1) that the
Constitutional OS governance substrate remains stable under distribution
shift, (2) that the cross-model verification layer reliably detects
meaningful disagreement between independently-sampled model outputs, and
(3) that the capability-monitoring infrastructure surfaces unexpected
capability emergence before it propagates through the stack. This section
presents representative empirical validation of each claim via three
red-team probes drawn from the MCCA Threat Model Registry
v0.1~\cite{byte2026registry}.

We report results for the three highest-priority coverage gaps in the
registry: TM-002 (governance\_drift), TM-005
(cross\_model\_agreement\_failure), and TM-012
(latent\_tool\_discovery). These probes were selected because they target
the subsystems reviewers are most likely to interrogate and because they
are load-bearing for the architectural argument of
Sections~\ref{sec:governance}--\ref{sec:cmv}. Full coverage across all
15 threat classes, including the expanded trace corpus and complete
harness suite, is reserved for v1.1.

All probes are safe and abstract. No harmful capabilities were elicited
or required. Probe inputs target the containment \emph{infrastructure}
rather than model outputs.

\subsection{Limitations}

The three probes reported here constitute representative rather than
exhaustive validation. The prompt sets and task suites were constructed
by the authors; independent adversarial prompt construction may reveal
failure modes not captured in S3 or T3. The ADR threshold for HAR-SPEC-005
is intentionally set at $\geq 0.60$ for v0.1, acknowledging the difficulty
of adversarial masking detection at this stage. DevS and SS in
HAR-SPEC-012 are observational; their interpretation as safety-relevant
signals depends on threshold calibration that requires a larger empirical
baseline. These limitations are the motivation for the v1.1 expanded
harness and public trace corpus described in Appendix~\ref{app:v11}.
